Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 1, 2026

LifeFlow Bridge is an open-source Android app that lets you listen to Life Network podcasts in standard podcast players. This policy explains what data the app handles and how.

Data Collection

LifeFlow Bridge does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on external servers. There are no analytics, no tracking, no ads, and no third-party services.

Credentials

Your Life Network email and password are used solely to authenticate with the Life Network API. Credentials are stored locally on your device using Android's encrypted keystore (via expo-secure-store). They are never sent anywhere except directly to Life Network's servers at app.joinlifenetwork.com.

Local HTTP Server

The app runs a local HTTP server on localhost:8080 to serve podcast RSS feeds to your podcast player. This server is only accessible on your device — it does not accept connections from the network or the internet.

Network Requests

The app makes requests to:

  • Life Network API (app.joinlifenetwork.com) — to authenticate, fetch your podcast subscriptions, episode data, and signed audio URLs.

No other network requests are made.

Data Storage

All data is stored locally on your device:

  • Login credentials — encrypted keystore
  • Podcast feeds and audio URLs — in-memory only (cleared when the app stops)

Data Deletion

To delete all data stored by LifeFlow Bridge:

  • Tap Logout in the app to clear saved credentials, or
  • Uninstall the app to remove all data

Children's Privacy

This app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes

Any updates to this policy will be posted on this page with an updated date.
